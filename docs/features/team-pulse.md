## The Problem

You ask "How's the team doing?" and get "Fine." Meanwhile, one developer is drowning in 12 open PRs, another hasn't committed in a week, and your bus factor on the core module is exactly one person. You don't find out until someone quits or burns out.

---

## The Solution

<div class="screenshot" markdown>
![Team Pulse — Team workload analysis with AI insights](../assets/screens/flavors/pm/team-pulse.png)
</div>

Team Pulse gives you an **honest, data-driven view** of your team's health. Every contributor shows their role, workload status, commit/PR/review counts, and focus area. JP's AI insights flag burnout risks and capacity imbalances before they become problems.

!!! success "Impact"
    **Prevent burnout, balance workloads, and build a healthier team.** No more surprises when someone is quietly drowning.

## What You See

<div class="feature-grid" markdown>

<div class="feature-card" markdown>
### :material-account-check: Workload Status
Each contributor tagged as **Balanced**, **Overloaded**, **Light**, or **Heavy** — based on actual commit, PR, and review activity.
</div>

<div class="feature-card" markdown>
### :material-fire: Burnout Detection
JP flags contributors showing burnout signals: sustained high output, decreasing review participation, or unusual work-hour patterns.
</div>

<div class="feature-card" markdown>
### :material-graph: Collaboration Graph
Visual map of **who reviews whom**. Spot isolated contributors, review bottlenecks, and opportunities to strengthen knowledge sharing.
</div>

<div class="feature-card" markdown>
### :material-brain: AI Insights
Smart recommendations: *"Alex has reviewed 80% of all PRs this sprint — consider spreading review load"* or *"No one else has committed to the auth module — bus factor risk."*
</div>
