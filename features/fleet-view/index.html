    Fleet View¶

    Monitor all your projects at once — a multi-project health overview ranked by risk score. One screen, all projects, highest risk first.

    View Jean-Pierre Flavor → Download AgentOS

    The Command Center for Managers¶

    Fleet View gives managers a bird's-eye view of all tracked projects. No more checking each project individually — see everything at once, ranked by urgency.

    What You See¶

    Health Score¶

    AI-calculated from PR activity, sprint progress, commit velocity, and blocker count. Instantly know which projects are thriving and which need attention.

    Risk Level¶

    Color-coded risk indicators: 🟢 Low · 🟡 Medium · 🔴 High — prioritize your attention based on real-time data analysis.

    Key Metrics¶

    Open PRs, active sprint items, recent commits, and more — the most important numbers at a glance for every project.

    Quick Actions¶

    Jump to any project's dashboard, ask JP for analysis, or trigger a report — all from the Fleet View without context-switching.

    Built For¶

    👨‍💼

    Engineering Managers¶

    Monitor 5–20 repos across teams at a glance. Identify risks before they escalate.

    🏢

    CTOs & VPs¶

    Executive overview of all active projects. Drill into any project for details.

    💻

    Tech Leads¶

    See which projects need your attention this week. Prioritize based on data, not gut feeling.

    🚀

    Freelancers¶

    Manage multiple client projects from one hub. Never lose track of a deliverable again.

    AI-Powered Fleet Intelligence¶

    Fleet View isn't just a list — it's an intelligent portfolio analyzer:

    • Cross-project risk analysis — JP identifies risks that only emerge when looking across projects
    • Velocity trending — spot team slowdowns before they impact delivery
    • Proactive alerts — stale PRs, sprint bottlenecks, and dependency conflicts surfaced automatically
    • One-click reports — generate portfolio-wide status reports for leadership in seconds

    Accessing Fleet View¶

    Click Fleet in the sidebar, or press ++cmd+k++ and type "fleet".
